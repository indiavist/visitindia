FASTIVAL

AFastival is an event orinarily celebrated by a community and centering on some characteristic aspect of that community and its cultures.

Durga Puja

Durga puja,major festival of Hinduism,teaditionally held for 10 days in the month of september-october,the seventh month of the Hindu calender,and particular celebrated in bengal,assam,and others eastern india states.Durga puja celebrates the victory of the goddes durga over the demong king Mahishasura.It begins on the same day as Navratri

Chhath Puja

Chhath is an ancient Hindu Vedic festival historicaliy native to the India subcontinent more specifically,the India States of Bihar,Jharkhand and Uttar pradesh and the Madhesh region of Nepal.The Chhath Puja is dedicated to the Solar deity(surya) in order to thank him for bestowing the bounties of life on earth and to request the granting of certain wishes.This festival is observed by Biharis & Nepalese along with their diaspore.

Eid

Eid al-fitr,also called the "Festival of Breaking the fast",is a religious holiday celebrated by Muslims worldwide that marks the end of the month-long dawn-to-sunset fasting of Ramadan.This religious Eid is the only day in the month of Shawwal during which Muslims are not pemitted to fast.The date for the start of any lunar Hijir month varies based on when the new moon is sighted by local religious authorities,so the day of celebration varies by locality.The holiday is widely called KORITE in west Africa.

Chrismas

Chrismas is a annual festival commermorating the birth of Jesus Chirist,observed primatily on December 25 as a religious and cultural celebration by million of people around the world.A feast central to the Christion liturgical year,it is preceded by the season of Advent or the Nativity fast and initiates the season of Christmastide,which historically in the west lasts twelve days and culminates on twelfth night.Christmas Day is a public holiday in many of the world's nations,is celebrate religious by a majority of christians,as well as culturally by many non-christians ,and froms an integral part of the holiday season centertd around it.

Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan,also Rakhi,is a popular,traditionally Hindu,annual rite,or ceremony,which is centerl to a festival of the same name,celebrate in india,Nepal,and other parts of the indian subcontinent,and among people around the world influnce by Hindu cultural.On this day,sisters of all ages tie a talisman,or amulet called the rakhi,around the wrists of their brothers,symbolically protecting them,receiving a gift in return,and traditionally investing the brothers with a share of the responsibility of their potential care.

Dewali

Diwali,is a festival of lights and one of the major festival celebrated by Hindus,jains and sikhs.The festival usually lasts five days and ic celebrated during the Hindu lunisolar month karkika.One of the most popular festival of Hinduism ,Diwali symbolizies the spiritual "victory of light over darkness,good over evil,and knowledge over ignorance.The festival is widely associated with laxmi,goddess of posperity ,with many oter riligional traditional conecting the holiday to Sita &Rama,vishnu,krishna yama,durga ,kali,hanuman or visvakara.

Dol Purnima

Dol Purnima,is a major holi festival of the india state of west bengal,Odisha,and Assam.This festival is dedicated to sri krisna and mainly celebrate by gopal community of odisha.On this auspicious day,an idol of krishna and his beloved Radha,Richly adorned and besmeared with colored poweder is taken out in precession in a swinging palanquin,decorated with flowers ,leaves,coloured cloths and papers.The procession proceeds forward to the accompaniment of music, blaring of conch shells,trumpets and shouts of joi and hari bol in odisha.

Buddha Purnima

Buddha Purnima is a festival that is celebrated in most of East asia commemorating the birth of prince Siddhartha Gautama,later the gautam buddha,who was the founder of Buddhism.According to buddhist tradition and modern academic consensus,Gautama buddha was born in lumbiniand raised in the Shakya capital of kapilavastu.

Pongal

Pongal is a Tamil harvest festival of South India , particular in the Tamil community.It is observed at the start of month Tai according to Tamil solar calender ,and this is typically about January 14.It is dedicated to the hindu sun god,the Suriya,and corresponds to Makar Sankranti ,the harvest festival under many regional names celebrate throughout India.The three day of the Pongal Festival are called Bhogi Pongal,Suriya Pongal.Some Tamils celebrate a fourth day of pongal as Kanum Pongal.
